About the Barangay

A little history, where to find us, and a look at Bag-ong Lapasan on the map.

History

Barangay Lapasan used to be a large community in terms of territory — in the past, it included what are now the barangays of Camaman-an and Indahag, before these were made regular barangays of Cagayan de Oro City. According to historical literature and conventional beliefs, the earliest inhabitants of Barangay Lapasan were people from the Bukidnon and Higaonon tribes.

Upon the order of President Diosdado Macapagal, a resettlement program was implemented, marking the arrival of people from the Visayas, particularly Boholano traders, who then settled in Lapasan. The Boholanos became the area's first settlers, while the generally shy Bukidnon and Higaonon moved to the interior areas now called Indahag and Camaman-an.

The word "Lapasan" is believed to be derived from the word "lapas," meaning to cross. This theory holds because Barangay Lapasan, at the time, had no source of fresh, potable water for drinking and cooking except when it rained. Early settlers had to fetch fresh water from the Cagayan River, and in doing so, had to cross a creek — now called Bitan-ag Creek — the boundary on the western side of Lapasan. That is how Barangay Lapasan got its name.

From these roots, Lapasan has grown into a bustling, highly urbanized barangay in the eastern part of Cagayan de Oro City, now home to government offices, a hospital, a university, and a growing number of local businesses and schools.

Location

Barangay Lapasan sits in the eastern part of Cagayan de Oro City, within the city's 2nd legislative district, and is one of the most populous barangays in the city. It is bordered by neighboring communities such as Sta. Cruz and San Juan, and is easily reached via Lapasan Hi-way, one of the city's main thoroughfares. The Barangay Hall, along Lapasan Hi-way, serves as the center of local governance and community services.
